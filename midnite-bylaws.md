I. The Group 

Purpose 

The Midnite Group is an organized group with a non-discriminatory membership that  hosts Alcoholics Anonymous meetings each week with various formats, in person and  virtually, throughout the afternoon and evening, seven (7) days a week and three  hundred sixty-five (365) days per year.  

Members 

The Midnite Group does not keep any formal membership roles as a means of  preserving anonymity as well as to maintain consistency with the Third Tradition of  Alcoholics Anonymous which states that “The only requirement for AA membership is a  desire to stop drinking”. There are no dues or fees for membership, but we do  encourage modest donations in order that the group may be self-supporting. Members  are encouraged to participate in service opportunities such as serving as meeting chairs,  overalls, and literature reps; group officers; and outgoing commitments to treatment  centers and other AA groups.

II. Meeting Level Service Policy and Procedure 

Meeting Policy and Procedure 

• Chairs should follow the approved script. 

• Meetings should begin on time. A Chair, Overall, Spiritual Bouncer, or a designated  current service person should remain in the room at all times. Service people are  expected to show up for service at least 15 minutes prior to the meeting start time. It is  the Service Person’s responsibility to try to find a replacement.  

• It is recommended that Service Persons share contact details in case of emergency to  signal an absence.  

• Meetings should close with the Serenity Prayer.  

**Additional Virtual Policy and Procedure** 

• Virtual meetings do not use waiting rooms or breakout rooms. The exception to this rule  is the Women’s and Men’s meetings which may use the waiting room to pre-screen. The  Chair or Spiritual Bouncer of the Men’s or Women’s meeting may also take an Individual  from the waiting room into a breakout room to explain the format of the meeting.  

• Virtual meetings may engage the Chat feature before and after the meeting, at the  Chair’s discretion.  

• Chat should not be engaged for participants during the meeting, although it may be  used by Service Persons to post information such as donation links, reading links, and  other Midnite related information.  

• It is important that someone monitor the Chat content. 

**Speaker Policy** 

• Have at least 90 days continuous sobriety. 

• Have not spoken at a Midnite in-person meeting within 90 days to speak at a Midnite in person meeting. 

• Have not spoken at a Midnite virtual person meeting within 90 days to speak at a  Midnite virtual meeting. 

• Should not engage in cross-talk or feedback after they have completed their qualification.  

**Sharing policy** 

• In general, sharing is limited to 3 minutes.  

• In case of a small meeting, a longer share time may be allowed at the Chair’s discretion.  • In-person rooms use a bell to signal it is time to wrap it up.

Disturbance Policy 

**In-Person Disturbance Policy** 

Crimes:  

The following incidents that occur in the Midnite meeting room or in the vicinity outside  of the room are considered **violations of the Criminal Code of the City of New York** and  **will not be tolerated**. **No warnings will be issued.** The Chair and/or Overall Chair will  ask the person to leave the room or the vicinity outside the room immediately following  the incident. If the person does not leave, or leaves and returns to the meeting, the  Chair and/or Overall Chair will call 911 to have the person removed. 

These crimes include, but are not limited, to: 

• **Assault or battery** (violence or unwanted physical contact) of an individual Group  individual(s). 

• **Theft** from an individual individual(s) or from the Group as a whole. • **Sexual harassment** of an individual Group individual(s). 

• **Verbal intimidation or threats** to an individual individual(s) or to the Group as a whole. • **Vandalism** of the meeting space or of the property of an individual individual(s)  attending a meeting. 

• **Public health risks** (i.e., communicable diseases, pests, etc.). 

• Stalking/Restraining Orders. 

• Our common welfare comes first; personal recovery depends on AA unity. Please  respect all Chair and Overall Chairperson’s decisions. 

Disturbances:  

The following incidents that occur in the Midnite meeting room or in the vicinity outside  of the room are considered **Disturbances** and **will not be tolerated**. The Chair and/or  Overall Chair will **issue one warning** to stop. If the person does not stop, the Chair  and/or Overall Chair have the option to ask the person to leave the room or the vicinity  outside the room for the duration of the meeting. If the person does not leave, or  leaves and returns to the meeting, the Chair and/or Overall Chair have the option to call  911 to have the person removed. 

These disturbances include but are not limited to: 

• Public consumption or display of illegal drugs or alcohol. 

• Panhandling in or outside the room.

AA Meeting Formats 

• Beginner’s Meetings focus on beginner’s topics and have tiered sharing. These meetings  are open to all. 

• Big Book meetings read from the basic AA text, Alcoholics Anonymous. • Closed Discussion Meetings feature a speaker. 

• Incoming speakers from other groups are featured at the Wednesday 6:30 and the  Saturday 8PM meetings. 

• Living Sober meetings read from the AA pamphlet Living Sober.  

• Meditation meetings incorporate a period of silent meditation in the meeting. • Men’s meetings are restricted to male-identifying members.  

• Open meetings mean anyone is allowed to attend, regardless of whether they identify  as alcoholic. Closed meetings are restricted to those who identify as having a desire to  stop drinking. 

• Open Pitch meetings are round robin sharing.  

• Relationships In Sobriety is a special interest meeting to share issues associated with  forming and nurturing relationships. 

• Step Meetings focus on one of the Twelve Steps of AA, while the Step One meeting is  dedicated to the First Step.  

• Topic meetings without a speaker request suggestions from the floor, or provide a list to  choose from, for a topic or topics to guide the content of the shares.  

• Topic meetings with a speaker request the speaker close with a topic of their choice to  guide the content of the shares.  

• Women’s meetings are restricted to female-identifying members.  • Dealers Choice (Virtual Only) allows Chair to select any on the above meetings at the  start of a new term for use for that complete term. 

Special Meetings

It meets on the  final Saturday of the month at 10 pm. By Group Conscience, this meeting may be  rescheduled as necessary. These meetings are open to all. 

• There is a special Group Anniversary meeting (celebrating the anniversary of the  Midnite Group’s establishment) held every June. 

• There is a also Holiday Party held every December.  

o These two meetings are scheduled at the Business Meeting several months prior to  the actual celebration.

IV. Group Level Service/Policy and Procedure 

Bylaws amendments 

• A motion for a change in the bylaws is introduced at a full group quarterly business  meeting. The motion for proposed change must be seconded and approved for  consideration. 

• The motion will also be posted on the website, posted as an addendum with the  minutes both virtually and in person, and emailed to the group. 

• Discussion occurs in the two interim virtual and in-person monthly business meetings • The motion will then be voted on in the following full-group quarterly business meeting.  • The original motion may be adjusted with comments and suggestions from the virtual  and in person monthly business meetings. 

Meeting Formats 

Meeting formats may be modified from time to time. The procedure consists of:  • Presenting the suggested change to the group at either an In-Person or Virtual business  meetings for discussion. 

• Approved at either an In-Person or Virtual business meeting. 

• Brought to the Quarterly business meeting for Midnite Group approval.  • After approval, it is adopted immediately. 

Meeting Scripts 

Meeting scripts may be modified from time to time. The procedure consists of: • Presenting the suggested change to the group at either an In-Person or Virtual business  meetings for discussion. 

• Approved at either an In-Person or Virtual business meeting. 

• Brought to the Quarterly business meeting for Midnite Group approval.  • After approval of the new script, it is adopted immediately. 

Announcements 

Announcements are not considered part of the official script. Group announcements are  added to the secretary report by a Group Overall and Secretary. At In-Person meetings,  Individual groups members may make AA-related announcements. At Virtual meetings,  individuals may make AA-related announcements in the chat after the meeting is over.

Income 

**Collections**  

In keeping with AA’s Seventh Tradition which states that “Every AA group ought to be  fully self-supporting, declining outside contributions”, the Midnite Group’s primary  source of income is voluntary member contributions.  

• No person is ever turned away from any meeting if they are unable to contribute.  • There are two collections taken at each meeting.  

• Any individual contributions made between members including loans, donations, or  gifts, while not expressly forbidden, are considered out of the scope of the group and  are not officially recognized by the group in any way.  

• From time to time, by Group Conscience, special voluntary collections can be  authorized, such as defraying the cost of the burial of a deceased group member, or AA related fund such as literature for institutions.  

• In addition to collecting cash at in-person meetings, the group also collects seventh  tradition donations via a Dipjar, located in the room at 220 West Houston St, and via  online methods such as Venmo, Paypal, etc. 

**Literature/Merchandise**

Operating Procedures 

The Corporation account will be funded with excess of the prudent reserve of the Group  account. The Group account will retain no more than the three months operating  expenses. Upon written or printed request from the Group Treasurer or Group Overall  Chair, the Corporation will disburse the funds to the Group account. At any time that  the Group’s account balance is less than one month’s expenses, the Group Overall or  Group Treasurer will instruct the Corporation to fund the Group account to restore  between one and three months operating expenses.
